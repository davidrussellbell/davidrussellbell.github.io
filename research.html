Machine Learning, Chemistry, Physics, and Biology

While at IBM, I also had the opportunity to help out for a program called Family Science Saturdays. In this program, ~10 year old students and there parents spend six Saturday mornings participating in different science and engineering activities. I helped out with the Intro to Electronics course, where we taught about binary and other numbering systems as well as logic gates, culminating in the construction of an adder on purpose-built electronic kits. I also had a blast with this program, especially exposing students to new numbering systems and working through the lab activities while keeping the parents engaged as well. Furthermore, I had a great set of mentors whom again I learned a great deal from.

Multiscale Modeling

Lastly, I was fortunate to serve as a judge for Westchester County and NYC high school science fairs. In this role students presented their science fair projects to me while I scored their projects and presentations. The best projects had nice results but really strong error quantification procedures and knowledge of the limitations of their work. Overall, I was quite impressed, and I enjoyed talking with young scientists about what they enjoyed and didn't enjoy about their projects.
