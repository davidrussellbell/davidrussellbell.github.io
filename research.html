Machine Learning, Chemistry, Physics, and Biology

Machine Learning (ML) and Artifical Intelligence (AI) techniques are powerful tools to explore and interrogate unsolved problems in Chemistry, Physics, and Biology. They are not magical perfect solvers for every problem (unfortunately!): they have their limitations and weaknesses. However, in my experience, when used appropriately, these techniques can add value to research projects. I am interested in ML techniques for exploring natural and synthetic molecules. This includes modeling structure, dynamics, interactions, and function. In my research statement, I propose several projects that incorporate ML, including GANs for multiscale modeling (in itself an explainable AI techinque in my opinion), Gaussian Processes for interpolating interactions, and time series models for dynamic prediction. I retain diverse interests in the burgeoning ML field, particularly moving beyond supervised learning problems and using ML in a generative capacity.
